Bitcoin surges to new all-time high amid easing macro headwinds
Bitcoin broke above its January peak on Wednesday, hitting a new record of $109,500 before easing slightly to $108,955, up 2% intraday, according to Coin Metrics. The surge marks a sharp rebound from early Aprils lows of $74,000.
Antoni Trenchev, cofounder of crypto exchange Nexo, attributes the move to softer U.S. inflation, a pause in U.S.-China trade tensions, and Moodys downgrade of U.S. sovereign debt, which have collectively boosted demand for alternative stores of value.
Bitcoin has gained 16% in May, driven by record ETF inflows now exceeding $40 billion, and rising Wall Street adoption. On-chain data also points to reduced selling pressure and a liquidity uptick, with record levels of Tether (USDT) parked on exchanges, signaling strong buying capacity.
Trenchev suggested a favorable three-month window may be opening for risk assets if geopolitical conditions continue to stabilize.
